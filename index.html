♥ Day 1 · Sat 11 Jul

Bath, then Bristol

Drop bags at luggage storage near Cardiff Central, then train to Bath for the morning. After lunch, a 15-min train to Bristol for the afternoon and evening.

Bath
Bath · paid
Roman Baths

2,000-year-old thermae with the famous open-air Great Bath. One of the best-preserved Roman sites in the world.

Official site ↗
Bath · free
Bath Abbey

Perpendicular Gothic church with soaring fan vaults and angels climbing ladders on its west front — free and right beside the Baths.

Official site ↗
Bath · free
Pulteney Bridge

One of only four bridges in the world lined with shops on both sides, arching over a beautiful horseshoe weir.

Visit Bath ↗
Bath · free outside
Royal Crescent & The Circus

Bath's grandest Georgian sweep — 30 terraced houses around a perfect lawn. Famous filming location for Bridgerton.

No.1 Museum ↗
Bath · paid · optional
Thermae Bath Spa

Rooftop open-air pool fed by the same ancient hot springs — swim with views over Georgian rooftops. Book in advance!

Official site ↗
Bath · small fee
Parade Gardens

Pretty Victorian riverside gardens right below Pulteney Bridge — sit by the weir and watch the world go by.

Visit Bath ↗
Bath · paid
Fashion Museum

Hundreds of years of dress history in the gorgeous Assembly Rooms — from Tudor garments to modern couture.

Official site ↗
Bath · National Trust
Prior Park Landscape Garden

18th-century hillside garden with a Palladian bridge and panoramic views down over Bath. Short bus ride out of town.

National Trust ↗
Bristol
Bristol · paid
SS Great Britain

Brunel's revolutionary 1843 iron ocean liner, preserved in the dry dock where she was built. A proper museum inside too.

Official site ↗
Bristol · small toll
Clifton Suspension Bridge

Brunel's iconic span over the Avon Gorge. Bus up, walk back down through charming Clifton village.

Official site ↗
Bristol · free
Bristol Harbourside

The old Floating Harbour now buzzing with bars, galleries and moored boats — beautiful to walk along in the evening.

Visit Bristol ↗
Bristol · free · open Sat
M Shed

Bristol's story through docks, people and culture. Free to enter and right on the harbourside. Closed Mondays — today's your day.

Official site ↗
Bristol · free · open Sat
Bristol Museum & Art Gallery

Free — dinosaurs, Egyptian mummies, fine art and natural history. Closed Mondays, so catch it today.

Official site ↗
Bristol · free
Cabot Tower & Brandon Hill

Free Victorian tower with panoramic views over Bristol. The park is a lovely spot to sit on the grass afterwards.

Visit Bristol ↗
Bristol · free

Bristol's oldest covered market — street food, crafts and independent traders. Great for a quick bite before heading back.

Official site ↗
Bristol · free

Iconic stencil murals scattered around Stokes Croft and Bedminster. Pick up a free map from the tourist info and wander.

Visit Bristol ↗
Bristol · free
Clifton Village

Upmarket Georgian neighbourhood near the bridge — independent boutiques, cafés and gorgeous views down into the Avon Gorge.

♥ Day 2 · Sun 12 Jul

Cardiff city centre

Nearly everything below is walkable. Cardiff Bay is a short Cardiff Bus ride or a lovely 30-min riverside stroll south.

City centre
Cardiff · paid
Cardiff Castle

Roman fort, Norman keep and lavish Victorian Gothic mansion all layered on one site right in the city centre.

Official site ↗
Cardiff · free · Tue–Sun
National Museum Cardiff

World-class free galleries — dinosaurs, Monet, Van Gogh, natural history. Open today (Sun) but closed Monday — don't miss it!

Official site ↗
Cardiff · free
Bute Park & Arboretum

56 acres of parkland threading along the Taff river right behind the castle. Perfect for a slow, peaceful wander.

Official site ↗
Cardiff · free
Victorian Arcades

Four gorgeous glass-roofed Victorian lanes — Royal, Morgan, Wyndham and Castle — packed with indie shops and cosy cafés.

Cardiff Market ↗
Cardiff · paid tour
Principality Stadium

One of the world's great rugby stadiums with a retractable roof — walking distance from the castle. Tours run daily.

Cardiff · free to browse

Victorian covered market since 1891 with fresh produce, Welsh cakes, street food and quirky stalls.

Striking slate-and-steel arts building on the waterfront. Wander the free foyer or catch a show if one's on.

Dramatic glass parliament building by Richard Rogers. Free access to the public gallery — the architecture alone is worth the trip.

The main promenade of Cardiff Bay — restaurants, cafés and open water views. A lovely spot for late afternoon coffee.

Official site ↗
Cardiff Bay · free

Cardiff's iconic Victorian building in terracotta — nicknamed the Welsh Big Ben. Free to visit and photograph up close.

Official site ↗
Cardiff Bay · free

Walk across the barrage for open views of the bay and the Bristol Channel beyond. Great for photos on a clear day.

♥ Day 3 · Mon 13 Jul

Out of Cardiff + the flight

Recommended: Caerphilly morning → Castell Coch afternoon. Back by 18:00 for dinner before the flight. St Fagans and Penarth are free/easy alternatives.

Day trips
Morning · 20 min train
Caerphilly Castle

Largest castle in Wales with massive moats and its very own leaning tower. Recently restored with new digital exhibits. Train ticket gets 2-for-1 entry!

Official site ↗
Afternoon · 30 min bus
Castell Coch

A fairy-tale Victorian Gothic castle hiding in beech woods. All rooms open despite conservation works. No café on site — bring snacks. Bus 132 from Cardiff.

Official site ↗
Alternative · free · 20 min bus
St Fagans National Museum

One of Europe's best open-air museums — 40+ historic Welsh buildings across 100 acres of grounds. Free entry, open Mondays. Bus 32/320 from Cardiff Central.

Official site ↗
Alternative · 15 min train
Penarth Pier & Seafront

Classic Victorian pier town just 15 mins from Cardiff — seaside walk, views over the Bristol Channel, and an art deco pavilion on the pier.

Visit Wales ↗
Evening back in Cardiff
Cardiff · free
Roath Park Lake

Cardiff's most beloved park — a long boating lake with a lighthouse memorial to Captain Scott. Quiet and green, close to the city.

Cardiff Council ↗
Cardiff Bay · free
Cardiff Bay at dusk

If the timing allows, the Bay in the evening is magical — the WMC is lit up and the barrage makes for a calm last walk before the airport.

Cardiff Harbour ↗
Cardiff · dinner
Chippy Lane & City Eats

Cardiff's legendary Caroline Street — chip shops, kebab spots and quick bites since the 1920s. Classic pre-airport fuel.
